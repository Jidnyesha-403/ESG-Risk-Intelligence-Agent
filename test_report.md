**ESG RISK INTELLIGENCE REPORT: Tesla**
=====================================

**Executive Summary**
-------------------

This report provides an analysis of Tesla's ESG risks, highlighting key areas of concern and comparing them to historical industry benchmarks. Our analysis reveals a high environmental risk level, medium social risk level, and low governance risk level. We recommend that Tesla's management prioritize addressing these risks to maintain a strong ESG profile.

---
**Environmental (E) Risks**
-------------------------

* **Current Findings:** Tesla faces high environmental risks due to concerns around greenhouse gas (GHG) footprint, methane leakages, and environmental degradation. [3], [5], [8], [9], [10]
* **Historical Industry Benchmark:** The Energy, Oil & Gas ESG Benchmark (2025-2026) requires a concrete transition plan to net-zero by 2045, methane leakage rates below 0.15% of throughput, and minimal water footprint. Tesla's current practices do not meet these standards.
* **Comparison:** Tesla's environmental risks are higher than the industry benchmark, indicating a need for improvement in transition planning, methane leakage control, and water footprint management.

---
**Social (S) Risks**
------------------

* **Current Findings:** Tesla's social risks are medium, with concerns around worker health and safety, and transition-risk planning. [1], [2], [4], [7], [8]
* **Historical Industry Benchmark:** The Energy, Oil & Gas ESG Benchmark (2025-2026) requires zero fatalities in operations and robust local community outreach. Tesla's current practices meet these standards, but there is room for improvement in transition-risk planning.
* **Comparison:** Tesla's social risks are lower than the industry benchmark, indicating a strong focus on worker safety and community engagement.

---
**Governance (G) Risks**
----------------------

* **Current Findings:** Tesla's governance risks are low, with concerns around executive compensation tied to decarbonization targets. [6], [7], [10]
* **Historical Industry Benchmark:** The Energy, Oil & Gas ESG Benchmark (2025-2026) requires executive compensation tied to decarbonization targets. Tesla's current practices meet these standards.
* **Comparison:** Tesla's governance risks are lower than the industry benchmark, indicating a strong focus on decarbonization targets and executive accountability.

---
**Strategic Recommendations**
---------------------------

1. Develop a concrete transition plan to net-zero by 2045 and implement methane leakage control measures to reduce environmental risks.
2. Enhance transition-risk planning and community engagement to address social risks.
3. Review executive compensation structures to ensure alignment with decarbonization targets and industry benchmarks.
